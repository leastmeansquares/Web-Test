An EPC tells you how energy efficient the property is. EPCs rate a home from A (very efficient) to G (inefficient). They’ll tell you how costly it will be to heat and light your property, and what its carbon dioxide emissions are likely to be. The EPC report also includes information on what the energy efficiency rating could be if you made the recommended improvements. It also highlights cost effective ways to improve your EPC rating. Even if you rent out the property, you should still try to make the recommended improvements noted on the EPC, such as switching to more energy efficient light bulbs, loft insulation, cavity wall insulation (where possible), double glazing, etc An EPC report is valid for 10 years from the date of issue. Please keep up to date with any changing legislation and stay ahead of the game when it comes remaining compliant. Ask us if you are unsure about anything.
As of 1st April 2021, every tenancy in England requires a valid EICR certificate. Landlords must supply tenants with a copy of a valid EICR at the beginning of the tenancy; upon issuing a new contract (incl. renewals); and upon request from any tenant, within 28 days of receiving a written request. We can arrange this on your behalf if you do not have a valid EICR.
